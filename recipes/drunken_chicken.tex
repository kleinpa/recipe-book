\recipe{Drunken Chicken}

Slow cooker Yats-inspired\footnote{via https://oldworldgardenfarms.com/2017/05/02/drunken-chicken-recipe} Drunken Chicken.

\recipesection{Recipe}

Make light light roux (until slightly nutty smelling). Heat oil over medium heat and slowly add flour. Stir constantly.

\begin{ingredients}
\ingredient{112}{g}{vegetable oil}
\ingredient{65}{g}{flour}
\end{ingredients}

Add onion, garlic, pepper and continue to saute until onion is transparent.

\begin{ingredients}
\ingredient{1}{}{onion}
\ingredient{1}{tsp}{minced garlic}
\ingredient{1}{}{poblano}
\end{ingredients}

Add dry spices and mix.

\begin{ingredients}
\ingredient{1}{tbsp}{garlic powder}
\ingredient{1}{tbsp}{thyme}
\ingredient{1}{tsp}{black pepper}
\ingredient{2}{tsp}{cayenne pepper}
\ingredient{1}{tsp}{oregano}
\ingredient{1}{tsp}{paprika}
\ingredient{1}{tbsp}{salt}
\ingredient{1}{tbsp}{molasses}
\end{ingredients}

Slowly add half of the diced tomato. Then add chicken, beer, and bread crumbs then gently mix. Cover with remaining diced tomato and add bay leaves.

\begin{ingredients}
\ingredientAlt{850}{g}{30}{oz}{diced tomatoes}
\ingredientAlt{355}{ml}{12}{fl oz}{beer}
\ingredientAlt{800}{g}{1.75}{lb}{boneless, skinless chicken thighs/breasts}
\ingredient{60}{g}{italian style bread crumbs}
\ingredient{4}{}{bay leaves}
\end{ingredients}

Slow cook for 4 hours or until the chicken is very tender. Shred chicken and cook for one more hour. Serve over basmati rice or with garlic bread.
